💛 GiGi’s Playhouse Volunteer Opportunities

Down Syndrome Achievement Center

GiGi’s Playhouse offers an incredible, hands-on opportunity for high school students to help deliver free educational, therapeutic, and career development programs for individuals of all ages with Down syndrome. This organization is almost entirely volunteer-run and relies on enthusiastic peers to help celebrate achievements and build a world of total acceptance.

It is a perfect pathway for students who want to explore fields like pediatric medicine, physical/occupational therapy, special education, or speech-language pathology while making an immediate, positive impact.

🔍 What Do Volunteers Actually Do?

As a volunteer, you will help facilitate purposeful activities designed to build motor, social, and literacy skills. Depending on your availability and interests, your role can include several interactive projects:

Project Area What You Can Do
Program Assistant Supporting the Program Leader during group sessions by greeting families, helping set up materials, and encouraging participants to fully engage in games and exercises.
Literacy & Math Tutor Working one-on-one with a specific student for 1 hour per week during a 10-week session, using tailored curriculum guides to help them hit their reading or math goals.
Teen Tastic & Socials Joining social groups for teens and young adults (ages 13–17) to act as a peer mentor, participate in team-building activities, and model positive social interactions.
GiGiFIT & Motor Skills Assisting with fitness, dance, or movement programs designed to improve strength, coordination, and universal physical health.
Playhouse Clean Team Coming in to help organize, decorate, and clean the Playhouse to keep it a safe, beautiful, and welcoming environment for families.

⏱️ Important Rules to Know Before You Apply

  • Age & Permission: If you are between 15 and 17 years old, you can attend your shifts completely on your own, but you will need a parent or guardian to sign a standard permission waiver. If you are 18 or older, you can sign your own paperwork.
  • Background Checks: To protect the safety of all participants and families, the organization requires a standard background check for volunteers.
  • Commitment & Consistency: Because individuals with Down syndrome thrive on routines and familiar faces, showing up consistently for your scheduled sessions is incredibly important.

📋 Step-by-Step Application Roadmap

Getting started with GiGi’s Playhouse is structured around a clear onboarding process. You can complete the initial steps at your own pace to get familiar with the Playhouse model! Take it one step at a time.

  • Step 1: Submit the Online Profile — Visit the local GiGi’s Playhouse volunteer portal and fill out the digital volunteer interest application, noting your availability and specific interests.
  • Step 2: Attend a Volunteer Orientation — Sign up for a 1-hour volunteer orientation session (listed on their online calendar) to learn about the mission, vision, and basic concepts regarding Down syndrome.
  • Step 3: Complete Onboarding & Training — Read through the provided training materials and submit your signed parental consent waiver.

    Specialized Tracks: If you are tracking toward one-on-one tutoring, you will complete an extra specialized curriculum training session here.

  • Step 4: Program Observation — Before jumping into a permanent role, you will schedule a time to visit the Playhouse, introduce yourself, and observe an active program session so you know exactly what to expect.
