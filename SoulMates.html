🦋 SoulMates Teen Volunteer Program

Hospice & Community Care

The SoulMates Program is a unique opportunity designed specifically for area high school students to learn firsthand about the impact they can have on the community. This program eliminates the need for prior clinical experience and focuses entirely on providing comfort, companionship, and emotional support to hospice patients and their families.

It is an ideal local pathway for students aged 15 to 18 years old who want to build meaningful intergenerational connections and explore healthcare or counseling fields.

🔍 What Do Volunteers Actually Do?

As a SoulMates volunteer, your focus is entirely on comfort, companionship, and human connection rather than medical tasks. Depending on your strengths and interests, your role can include a variety of meaningful projects:

Project Area What You Can Do
Patient Companionship Visiting with patients to talk, listen, or read together.
Life Reviews Recording patient interviews and documenting their life stories for their families.
The Sunshine Crew Getting involved with holiday crafts, making birthday cards, or baking treats for patients.
Music Support Playing an instrument or singing to patients and families to provide a peaceful environment.
Guest Services Welcoming visitors and families at the Bob Fryer & Family Inpatient Center in Mount Joy.
Campus & Event Help Assisting with local landscaping or gardening projects and supporting special community events.

⏱️ Important Rules to Know Before You Apply

  • Age Limits: This specific program is strictly for high school teens between 15 and 18 years old.
  • Training Commitment: Because working with hospice patients requires high sensitivity, you must attend a mandatory, comprehensive in-person volunteer training session. These interactive training sessions are offered quarterly throughout the year.

📋 Step-by-Step Application Roadmap

Getting started with this program is incredibly straightforward. Once you complete the initial form, the hospice volunteer coordinator will reach out to get you scheduled for training before you have to worry about completing any other paperwork! Take it one step at a time.

  • Step 1: Submit the Online Application — Visit the Hospice & Community Care portal and fill out the quick digital SoulMates interest form.
  • Step 2: Attend Training First — Complete the comprehensive quarterly training program with your coordinator. This gives you the exact tools and confidence you need to understand the role before finalizing your onboarding.
  • Step 3: Complete Final Requirements — After training is done, your coordinator will walk you through completing the remaining steps required to safely visit patients:

    Parental Consent Form: A signed permission sheet from a parent or legal guardian since you are a minor.

    Health Screenings: A routine Tuberculosis (TB) skin test (which is entirely paid for and provided by the organization at no cost to you) and an up-to-date flu vaccination.
