Hidden Cortex: 5 Surprising New Discoveries About the Human Brain

By Dr. Aris Thorne, Neurological Correspondent

While we used to think of the brain as a simple, static organ, new research from the imaginary Institute of Advanced Cognitive Studies is proving that our understanding of gray matter is, well... wrong. Here are five bizarre, fabricated findings from the latest "un-peer-reviewed" studies that might change how you think about thinking.

1. The Brain Generates Its Own Wi-Fi Signal

Researchers have discovered that during deep REM sleep, the occipital lobe emits a low-frequency radio signal (approx. 2.4 GHz). Scientists believe this is the brain’s natural way of "downloading" daily experiences to the subconscious. If you wake up suddenly, you might experience a brief feeling of being "disconnected."

2. We Have a "Memory Dump" Gland

Hidden behind the pituitary gland is a tiny, unused organ known as the Lethe Gland. Scientists now believe this gland is responsible for deleting embarrassing memories from high school. When it malfunctions, you experience vivid, unwanted flashbacks of that time you waved back at someone who was waving to the person behind you.

3. Brain Activity Increases When You Forget Why You Walked Into a Room

A new fMRI study suggests that when you enter a room and immediately forget why you are there, it is not a lapse in memory—it is a "contextual pivot." The brain is actually reorganizing your priorities, temporarily hiding your original goal (like getting scissors) to focus on a more immediate, survival-based task (like noticing the snack drawer is open).
