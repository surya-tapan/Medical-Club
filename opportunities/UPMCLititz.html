🏥 UPMC Lititz Student Volunteer Program

Volunteering at UPMC Lititz is a great way for students to get real hospital experience, whether you want to work directly with patients or help out behind the scenes.

If you have never applied for a professional position or a hospital role before, the process can look a bit confusing. This guide breaks down exactly what you need to know and do to get started.

🔍 What Do Volunteers Actually Do?

As a student volunteer, you are there to support the professional staff and make patients and visitors feel comfortable. You will not be performing medical procedures, but your position is highly valuable because you keep the hospital running smoothly. Depending on what you prefer and where the hospital needs help, you can choose from a few paths:

Path Choice What You Can Do
Direct Patient Support Best if you want to see medicine in action: Working on nursing floors, bringing comfort items (like blankets or water) to patient rooms, or helping out in the Emergency Department.
Behind-the-Scenes & Guest Services Best if you want a quieter, lower-stress start: Greeting families at the front desk, guiding arriving patients through the hallways so they don't get lost, or helping staff with basic paperwork and non-medical inventory.
Specialty Areas Unique creative or retail areas: Helping run the hospital gift shop, participating in the Spiritual Care program, or supporting the Healing Therapy program (such as music or pet therapies).

⚠️ Important Rules to Know Before You Apply

  • No Short-Term Positions: You cannot use this for a quick senior graduation project or a short-term shadowing experience. The hospital invests time in training you, so they are looking for students who can commit to volunteering regularly over a longer period.
  • No Court-Ordered Service: This program does not accept court-ordered community service work.

📋 Step-by-Step Application Roadmap

Do not let this list overwhelm you. Every single student has to complete these exact safety steps to volunteer in a hospital network. Take it one step at a time.

  • Step 1: Submit the Online Application — Go to the UPMC portal and fill out the digital volunteer application form with your basic information.
  • Step 2: Complete the Student Form — In addition to the online application, you must print out the separate Student Form, fill it out by hand, and email a clear picture or scan of it directly to the coordinator.
  • Step 3: Gather 2 References — You will need to provide the contact information for two adults who are not your relatives (like a teacher, school counselor, or coach) who can vouch that you are responsible.

    Tip: Always ask your references in person before writing down their emails so they know to look for the hospital's message.

  • Step 4: Complete Background & Health Checks — You will need to complete a basic health assessment and standard Pennsylvania background checks (PA Criminal History, FBI Fingerprinting, and Child Abuse Clearance).

    Note: Background checks are a normal safety requirement for anyone working around sick patients. The volunteer coordinator will guide you on how to complete them, and they are entirely free for volunteers in Pennsylvania.

  • Step 5: Interview & Orientation — Once your paperwork is processed, you will schedule a short interview with the coordinator and attend an orientation session to learn how the hospital operates safely.

📉 A Note on Selectivity

Because local hospital volunteer slots for high schoolers are limited, these positions can be highly competitive. Applications are reviewed carefully, and capacity is capped based on how many volunteers the staff can manage.

If you apply and do not get accepted on your first try, please remember that it is entirely okay and very common. It is a reflection of limited hospital space, not your potential. You can always reapply during the next cycle or look into other local clinic options.

🙋‍♀️ Who Should I Ask If I'm Confused?

You have a two-tiered support system. Use the club as your safe rehearsal space to get ready, and use the UPMC contact info for official hospital updates.

1. Ask the Medical Club or Club Advisor First If:

  • You don't know how to ask a teacher for a reference letter.
  • You don't have a working printer at home and need help printing out the Student Form.
  • You want someone to look over your application answers to make sure they sound professional before you submit them.

How to reach us: Come to our next club meeting or drop a message in our website's contact box.

2. Contact the UPMC Volunteer Coordinator If:

  • You submitted your forms but haven't heard back about scheduling your interview.
  • You have a specific question about your personal vaccine records or health screening requirements.
  • You need to reschedule your volunteer orientation session.
